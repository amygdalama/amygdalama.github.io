Answering Vague Interview Questions

November 30 2014

Interviewer: So, tell me about yourself.

Oh.

No.

Me: Um, hi, I'm Amy. I'm, uh, I studied math and then worked and then did Hacker School and yeah. Now I'm interviewing and stuff.

Pause.

Longer pause.

Death rattle.

Interviewer: Great. So I'm going to ask you a few technical questions...

Avoid this awful situation with this ONE SIMPLE TRICK: Don't talk about yourself!

For me, talking about myself is hard. But talking about things I'm interested in is really easy.

Instead, try immediately jumping into a project that you found really interesting, and a bug you found really interesting to solve. For example, I usually answered the dreaded "tell me about yourself" question like so:

I'm Amy. I studied Math in college and then worked in ad tech for a bit where I taught myself programming and machine learning. Then I went to Hacker School,

Okay, enough about me, let's get to the good stuff. A project I worked on and why I liked it:

where I worked on projects like replacing the import keyword in Python with accio, a Harry Potter spell. I enjoyed that project in particular because there wasn't a straightforward solution -- I had to edit the Grammar file in the CPython source code, and then compile the Python interpreter, and then see what unexpected SyntaxErrors and whatnot I'd get, and then rinse and repeat. The next step was always a mystery waiting to be solved.

As a person being interviewed, you have more control over the conversation that you may think. You want to give your interviewer plenty of hooks so that they can ask follow-up questions about things they find interesting too.
